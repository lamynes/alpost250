ABOUT POST 250

Charter Signed for American Legion Post 250, Fowl River, Alabama November 15, 1971.

On November 15, 1971, Fowl River, Alabama, was granted a Charter for the formation of an American Legion Post. The original charter hangs in the Hallway of Post 250. It was signed by the National Commander and Adjutant at National Headquarters, Indianapolis, Indiana. Listed on the document are the following names of men requesting a Post: James W. Haley, C.E. Hankin, C.D. Cooper, Joseph K. Pickett, James B Fincher, Jesse Eldridge, Roy E. Richardson, Herbert C. Smith, Frank L. Moffit Sr., Curtis L. Sullivan Jr., Thomas J. Murdoch Jr., Douglas E. Slay, Leslie T. Richardson, Patrick H. Foley, Henry Johnson, Dave A. Richardson, Harry C. Dickin, Capt. G. Adams, W. H. Lee, Carl Eldridge, Paul Everett and Wayne Thompson.

OUR MISSION

The American Legion was chartered and incorporated by Congress in 1919 as a patriotic veterans organization devoted to mutual helpfulness. It is the nation’s largest wartime veterans service organization, committed to mentoring youth and sponsorship of wholesome programs in our communities, advocating patriotism and honor, promoting strong national security, and continued devotion to our fellow service members and veterans.

Hundreds of local American Legion programs and activities strengthen the nation one community at a time. The Legion also raises millions of dollars in donations at the local, state and national levels to help veterans and their families during times of need and to provide college scholarship opportunities.


The American Legion is a nonpartisan, not-for-profit organization with great political influence perpetuated by its grass-roots involvement in the legislation process from local districts to Capitol Hill. Legionnaires’ sense of obligation to community, state and nation drives an honest advocacy for veterans in Washington. The Legion stands behind the issues most important to the nation’s veterans community, backed by resolutions passed by volunteer leadership.


The American Legion’s success depends entirely on active membership, participation and volunteerism. The organization belongs to the people it serves and the communities in which it thrives.

Veterans Service Officer (VSO)

Post 250 has two outstanding Veterans Service Officers, Rick Moritz and Dave Riley, who provide FREE advice and assistance to any veteran (you do not have to be a member of Post 250 to receive assistance). Our Veterans Service Officer provides VA benefits counselling (including disability benefits) and other assistance to veterans where funds and resources are available.


Contact Rick at (251)-591-8804 or Dave Riley at (251) 751-2537 to discuss your needs or make an appointment to meet.
